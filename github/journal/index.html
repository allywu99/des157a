Post #5: Game Design UX Best Practices Response

Hey there! I just read this awesome article on game design and UX best practices, and I wanted to share some of my thoughts with you.

The article does a great job of breaking down the key principles that game designers can use to create engaging and enjoyable experiences for users. One of the things that really stood out to me was the importance of creating a consistent and visually appealing design throughout the game. By using a consistent color palette, typography, and design language, designers can help users navigate the game and understand its mechanics more easily.

Another thing that the article touched on was the importance of making games accessible and inclusive to all users, regardless of their abilities or disabilities. This is such an important point, and it really highlights the need for designers to think carefully about the needs and preferences of their target audience.

I also appreciated the emphasis on testing and iterating on game designs based on user feedback. This user-centered approach can really help ensure that the game meets the needs and expectations of its audience, and can ultimately lead to a more successful and enjoyable game experience.

Overall, I found the article to be a really valuable resource for anyone interested in game design or UX. By incorporating these best practices into their designs, designers can create games that are not only fun and engaging, but also intuitive and user-friendly. Thanks for reading!

Post #2: Best Practices for Modals Response

Naema Basknderi's article on modal best practices reexamines its UI flaws. UI issues were elegantly solved when modal windows first appeared on the scene. The first is that it streamlines the user interface, and the second is that it conserves screen space. Since then, the modal window has been widely used by designers, some of whom have gone overboard. Today's equivalent of the dreaded pop-up window is a modal. Users have been educated to dismiss modal windows out of habit because they find them unpleasant.

It was interested to see that mobile devices and modals typically don't get along. Modals either take up too much screen space or are too small, making it impossible to see the content. Users are forced to pinch and zoom in order to see the fields of a modal window when the device keyboard and nested scrollbars are added. Modals are not appropriate for use on mobile devices and have superior alternatives.

Overall, I learned that modals have many advantages, such as drawing the user's attention, maintaining context, and streamlining the user interface. However, there are drawbacks since they obstruct user flow and prevent interaction with the parent page by concealing the information behind the modal. Modal might not always be the best choice. When choosing whether to use a Modal, it's best to take into account when to show it, how to show it, and what information is going to be presented.
